About APHont

APHont (American Printing House font) is specifically designed for readers with visual impairments. Key features:

  • Distinct character shapes to reduce confusion
  • Optimized spacing between letters and words
  • Available in Regular, Bold, Italic, and Bold Italic
  • Free for eligible users (non-commercial use)
APHont Sample: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.

Download APHont: Available from the American Printing House website.

About Tiresias Infofont

Developed by RNIB (Royal National Institute of Blind People), Tiresias is widely used for accessibility:

  • Excellent legibility at various sizes
  • Clear distinction between similar characters (1, I, l, etc.)
  • Free to use under GPL license
  • Commonly used in public information systems
Tiresias Sample: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.

Download Tiresias: Available from RNIB website or font repositories.

How to Share Your Accessible Text

Option 1: Download and Share Files

  1. Generate your text using the Font Generator
  2. Download as PNG (for images) or SVG (for scalable vector graphics)
  3. Upload to cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.) or email as attachment
  4. Send the download link to your recipient

Option 2: Generate Shareable Link

  1. Create your accessible text
  2. Click "Generate Shareable Link"
  3. Copy the provided URL
  4. Share via email, messaging apps, or social media

Option 3: Print for Physical Sharing

  1. Generate your text with appropriate size (minimum 18pt recommended)
  2. Use high-contrast color combinations
  3. Print on matte paper to reduce glare
  4. Consider using bold weight for better visibility
